Personalizing for a new generation of shoppers

Overview

Men’s wearhouse is a leader in traditional retail. To continue to grow, they need to reach a new, digital-first audience.

Role

Primary Designer

Challenge

Men’s Wearhouse wanted to explore how personalization (with significant technology constraints) could reach online shoppers.

Research

The strengths of the in-store sales process and the psychology of the emotional suit-shopping processs informed the design of an experience that needed to capture attention at every step.

  • Continuous Online Surveys
  • Ethnographic Study
  • Contextual Inquiries
The User's Journey

Personalization requires information-gathering from wary people. To work, the progression of questions needs to pre-empt customers’ natural ones.

The Result

The Right Questions At The Right Time

The winning flow hinges on moving from broad look to details and additions.

Mobile Experience

Because of significant mobile traffic, it was key for the flow to feel natural on small screens.

Feedback Through exhaustive remote user tests, surveys, and store employees, this personalization flow was validated and is being used as the foundation of TMW’s web personalization strategy.
