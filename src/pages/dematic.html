Making Data Simple Enough To Sell

Overview

Dematic is a global eader in providing warehouse automation solutions for Amazon, etc. Their sales teams were ready to work faster than their estimation software could.

Role

Primary Designer

Challenge

Prioritizing A World Of Possibilities

Dematic’s current solution was complicated and time-consuming to learn, dragging out the sales process over months and costing the company millions. Their product team spent months talking through a mountain of feature requests without being able to find consensus on the next step.

Research

The research process revealed the limitations of a spreadsheet approach to digesting complex datasets, and revealed three primary users the application needed to serve.

  • Competitive Analysis
  • User Interviews
  • Stakeholder Interviews
Design Sprint

I led a 5-day design sprint with Dematic’s many competing stakeholders to prototype and test a design solving their big problem - comparing sales “scenarios”.

The Result

FROM TABLES TO INTERFACE

Browse And Compare Framework

A Kanban-like approach to displaying data-objects allows for quicker comparison and more intuitive drilldown to details.

Action-Oriented Navigation

To keep new users from digging through many menus, the architecture is flat, with all core actions grouped together as simple buttons.

Powerful Asset Management

Visualizing project scenarios in a nested card structure lets users glance important metadata and creates a stronger visual tie to the data being displayed.

Team Change Tracker

An “updates” panel helps users always know what system updates or teammate changes are affecting their calculations, leading to faster troubleshooting.

Feedback Through user testing, users were able to complete all revelant activities in the new interface and 100% of users reported an increase in efficiency from before. My team was able to build trust with their organization and was given the contract to start redesigning other products in their suite.
